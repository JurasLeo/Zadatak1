HomeMade

Homemade dishes are meals prepared from scratch in the comfort of one's own kitchen, using fresh ingredients and personal recipes or family traditions. Here are some aspects and advantages of homemade dishes: Customization, Quality Ingredients, Healthier Options, Cost-Effectiveness, Family Bonding, Creativity and Experimentation

Kitchen Tips

Proper Food Storage

Keep frequently used kitchen utensils and cookware within reach, while storing rarely used items in less accessible areas. Use drawer organizers, shelves, and hooks to maximize space in the kitchen. Regularly clean kitchen surfaces and maintain tidiness to facilitate cooking.

Choosing Fresh Ingredients

Select fresh ingredients that are seasonal and locally available for the best flavor and nutritional value. When choosing fruits and vegetables, pay attention to their color, texture, and smell to ensure they are fresh.

Time Efficiency

Plan meals in advance and prepare multiple meals at once to save time and reduce stress during the week. Use free time to prepare and freeze meals that can be quickly reheated when you're busy.

Efficient Meal Planning

Make a weekly meal plan to streamline grocery shopping and ensure you have all the ingredients you need. Consider batch cooking staple ingredients like grains, beans, or proteins to use in multiple meals throughout the week.

Creamy chicken pesto pasta

Ingredients (9)

-350g Cucina Matese Penne Rigate
-300ml light cream
-1/2 cup basil pesto
-3 green onions, sliced
-1 1/2 cups skinless shredded roast chicken (see related recipe)
-1/2 cup drained sun-dried tomatoes, thinly sliced
-1/3 cup finely grated parmesan cheese
-Thinly sliced green onions, to serve
-Crusty bread, to serve

Steps


Step 1- Cook 350g Cucina Matese Penne Rigate in a saucepan of boiling, salted water, following packet directions, until tender. Drain, reserving 1/4 cup liquid.
Step 2- Return pasta to pan over medium-low heat. Add reserved liquid, 300ml light cream, 1/2 cup basil pesto, 3 green onions, sliced, 1 1/2 cups skinless shredded roast chicken (see related recipe) and 1/2 cup drained sun-dried tomatoes, thinly sliced. Stir to combine. Cook, stirring, for 1 to 2 minutes or until heated through.
Step 3- Divide between bowls. Top with 1/3 cup finely grated parmesan cheese and Thinly sliced green onions, to serve. Serve with Crusty bread, to serve.

Chicken and mushroom risotto

Ingredients(12)

-2.5L (10 cups) Massel chicken style liquid stock
-40g Coles Australian Salted Butter
-125ml (1/2 cup) olive oil
-2 brown onions, finely chopped
-880g (4 cups) Coles Arborio Rice
-1 1/2 tbsp fresh thyme leaves
-125ml (1/2 cup) dry white wine
-6 (about 500g) Coles RSPCA Approved Chicken Thigh Fillets, cut into 1cm pieces
-200g button mushrooms, cut into 1cm pieces
-4 garlic cloves, crushed
-120g (1 1/2 cups) finely grated parmesan
-Salt flakes, to season

Steps


Step 1- Bring the 2.5L (10 cups) Massel chicken style liquid stock just to the boil in a large saucepan. Reduce heat and hold at a gentle simmer.
Step 2- Heat 40g Coles Australian Salted Butter and 2 tablespoons of oil in a heavy-based stockpot or large flameproof casserole dish over medium heat. Add the 2 brown onions, finely chopped. Cook, stirring, for 5 minutes or until soft and translucent but not coloured. Add the 880g (4 cups) Coles Arborio Rice and 1 tablespoon of thyme. Cook, stirring, for 1 minute or until the grains appear slightly glassy.
Step 3- Add the 125ml (1/2 cup) dry white wine to the rice mixture and cook, stirring, until the liquid is absorbed. Add a ladleful (about 125ml/1/2 cup) of the simmering stock to the rice and stir constantly with a wooden spoon until the liquid is absorbed. Continue adding the stock mixture, a ladleful at a time, stirring constantly and allowing the liquid to be absorbed before adding the next ladleful, for 20-30 minutes or until the rice is tender yet firm to the bite and the risotto is creamy.
Step 4- Heat remaining oil in a large frying pan over high heat. Add the 6 (about 500g) Coles RSPCA Approved Chicken Thigh Fillets, cut into 1cm pieces and stir-fry for 5 minutes or until chicken just starts to brown. Add the 200g button mushrooms, cut into 1cm pieces and 4 garlic cloves, crushed. Cook for 2 minutes.
Step 5- Add the chicken mixture, 120g (1 1/2 cups) finely grated parmesan and remaining thyme to the risotto and combine. Season with Salt flakes, to season and pepper to serve.

Traditional roast lamb

Ingredients(8)

-2kg leg of lamb, fat trimmed
-1/4 cup olive oil
-1 tbsp chopped fresh rosemary leaves
-2 garlic cloves, crushed
-1.5kg chat potatoes
-Basic gravy (makes 2 to 2 1/2 cups)
-2 cups Massel beef style stock
-3/4 cup red wine
-2 1/2 tbsp Coles White Plain Flour

Steps


Step 1- Preheat oven to 200°C/180°C fan-forced. Lightly grease roasting pan. Place 2kg leg of lamb, fat trimmed in pan. Combine 1/4 cup olive oil, 1 tbsp chopped fresh rosemary leaves and 2 garlic cloves, crushed in a bowl. Rub half the oil mixture over lamb. Season with salt and pepper. Roast for 15 minutes.
Step 2- Reduce oven temperature to 180°C/160°C fan-forced. Roast lamb, basting with remaining oil mixture every 20 minutes, for 1 hour 15 minutes for medium or until cooked to your liking. Add 1.5kg chat potatoes to pan for last 40 minutes, turning halfway through cooking.
Step 3- Remove lamb from oven. Cover loosely with foil. Stand for 10 minutes. Carve. Serve with potatoes.
Step 4- Basic Gravy: Transfer meat (and any vegetables) to a plate to rest. Combine 2 cups Massel beef style stock and 3/4 cup red wine in a jug. Skim fat from roasting pan, leaving 1 1/2 tablespoons pan juices and fat in pan. Place pan over high heat. Add 2 1/2 tbsp Coles White Plain Flour. Cook, stirring with a wooden spoon, for 1 to 2 minutes or until mixture bubbles and becomes golden. Add juices from resting meat. Slowly add stock mixture to pan, stirring constantly. Cook, scraping pan, for 8 to 10 minutes or until thickened.
